# Micro-Encapsulation Technology

> The core innovation: protecting creatine monohydrate through thermal and acidic environments while enabling instant dissolution.

## The Problem

Standard creatine monohydrate in hot coffee:
- **Clumps** at the surface (hydrophobic shell formation)
- **Gritty texture** persists (incomplete dissolution)
- **Thermal degradation** potential (creatine â†’ creatinine conversion)
- **Acidic environment** stress (coffee pH 4.5-6.0)
- **Stirring required** for 30-60 seconds minimum

## The Solution: Triple Shield Technology

### Layer 1: Thermal Protection Shell
- **Material candidates:** Modified starch, maltodextrin, gum arabic
- **Function:** Creates heat barrier up to 100Â°C
- **Dissolve trigger:** Designed to release at specific temp threshold
- **Research priority:** Identify optimal coating that protects but releases

### Layer 2: pH Buffer Micro-Layer
- **Function:** Neutralizes local acidity during dissolution
- **Prevents:** Creatine-to-creatinine degradation pathway
- **Duration:** Active for 10-15 seconds (dissolution window)

### Layer 3: Rapid-Release Core
- **Particle size:** <50 microns (instant dispersion)
- **Surface treatment:** Hydrophilic coating for water attraction
- **Result:** Draws liquid in, releases payload, disperses
