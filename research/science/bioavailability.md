# Bioavailability Research

> Ensuring what goes in the cup actually gets absorbed and utilized by the body.

## Creatine Absorption Basics

### Absorption Pathway
1. **Ingestion** → Stomach (survives gastric acid)
2. **Small intestine** → Primary absorption site
3. **Blood plasma** → Transport to tissues
4. **Muscle uptake** → Via creatine transporters (CreaT1)
5. **Phosphorylation** → Conversion to phosphocreatine (ATP buffer)

### Known Bioavailability: ~99%
Creatine monohydrate is one of the most bioavailable supplements. This is why fancy forms (creatine HCL, buffered creatine, etc.) show no significant advantage in studies—monohydrate is already near-perfect.

## The Coffee + Creatine Question

**Short answer:** No significant evidence of interference.

**Detailed analysis:**

| Factor | Effect | Evidence |
|--------|--------|----------|
| Caffeine | No direct antagonism | Separate mechanisms |
| Polyphenols | Theoretical chelation | Not clinically significant |
| Chlorogenic acid | Minor pH effect | Buffered in GI tract |
| Drinking temperature | No effect on absorption | Only experience |

### The Caffeine + Creatine Myth

Old concern: "Caffeine negates creatine benefits"

**Reality check:**
- Based on one 1996 study with flawed methodology
- Multiple subsequent studies show no interference
- Millions of people take pre-workouts (caffeine + creatine) daily
- Combined use is industry standard

**FUSE position:** Coffee enhances the ritual without compromising the science.

## Factors That DO Affect Creatine Uptake

### 1. Insulin Response (Positive)
- Carbs/protein trigger insulin
- Insulin enhances muscle creatine uptake
- Coffee + breakfast = natural insulin spike
- **Implication:** Morning coffee context is ideal

### 2. Hydration Status
- Creatine pulls water into muscles
- Adequate hydration maximizes effect
- Coffee is mostly water
- **Implication:** Coffee contributes to fluid intake

### 3. Consistency
- Daily dosing > sporadic
- Morning habit = consistent timing
- **Implication:** Coffee ritual ensures compliance

### 4. Muscle Activity
- Active muscles take up more creatine
- Morning dose before daily activity = optimal
- **Implication:** Timing works with natural patterns

## Testing Protocol: Bioavailability Verification

### Option 1: Serum Creatine Study
1. Baseline blood draw (fasted)
2. Administer FUSE in coffee
3. Blood draws at 30, 60, 120 minutes
4. Compare to water-dissolved creatine control
5. Measure serum creatine levels

**Cost:** ~£2,000-5,000 for small n study
**Value:** Direct evidence, premium claim support

### Option 2: Urinary Creatinine Tracking
1. 24-hour urine collection baseline
2. 1 week FUSE supplementation
3. 24-hour urine collection post
4. Measure creatinine excretion increase
5. Indicates muscle creatine loading

**Cost:** ~£500-1,000
**Value:** Indirect but valid loading evidence

### Option 3: Third-Party Literature Citation
1. Reference existing creatine monohydrate studies
2. Demonstrate FUSE uses identical active ingredient
3. No new trials needed for baseline claims

**Cost:** £0
**Value:** Sufficient for launch claims

## Recommended Approach
