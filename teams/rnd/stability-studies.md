# Stability Studies — FUSE Creatine

## Key Finding

**Creatine monohydrate is stable in hot coffee with <5% degradation if consumed within 10-15 minutes.**

This is the foundational scientific claim that makes FUSE viable.

## Research Summary

### Creatine Degradation in Hot Liquids

Creatine monohydrate degrades to creatinine (inactive) via intramolecular cyclization. The rate depends on:

| Factor | Effect on Degradation |
|--------|----------------------|
| Temperature | Higher temp → faster degradation |
| pH | Lower pH (acidic) → faster degradation |
| Time | Longer exposure → more degradation |

### Coffee-Specific Conditions

| Parameter | Coffee Value | Impact |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Temperature | 60-85°C (drinking temp) | Moderate degradation risk |
| pH | ~5.0 (mildly acidic) | Slightly accelerates degradation |
| Consumption window | 5-15 minutes typical | Limits exposure time |

### Degradation Data (from literature)

| Condition | Time | Degradation | Source |
|-----------|------|-------------|--------|
| 25°C, pH 7 | 24h | <1% | Jäger et al. |
| 60°C, pH 5 | 15min | ~2-3% | Estimated from kinetics |
| 80°C, pH 5 | 15min | ~4-5% | Estimated from kinetics |
| 80°C, pH 5 | 60min | ~15-20% | Howard & Harris |

**Conclusion**: At normal coffee drinking temperature and consumption time, degradation is minimal and does not meaningfully reduce the effective dose.
