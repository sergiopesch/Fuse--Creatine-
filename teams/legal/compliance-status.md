# Compliance Status — FUSE Creatine

## Regulatory Overview

FUSE Creatine is classified as a **food supplement** under UK and EU regulations. Creatine monohydrate is an established ingredient — no Novel Foods application required.

## Compliance Areas

### 1. Product Classification

| Requirement | Status | Notes |
|-------------|--------|-------|
| Food supplement classification | ✅ Confirmed | Creatine monohydrate is established |
| Novel Foods assessment | ✅ Not required | Creatine is on EU positive list |
| GRAS status | ✅ Confirmed | Generally Recognized As Safe |

### 2. Health Claims (EU 1924/2006 & 432/2012)

| Claim | Status | Regulation |
|-------|--------|------------|
| "Increases physical performance in successive bursts of short-term, high intensity exercise" | ✅ Permitted | EU 432/2012 |
| "Daily intake of 3g creatine" condition | ✅ Must appear | EU 432/2012 |
| "Instant Fusion Technology" | ⚠️ Needs review | Proprietary claim — needs substantiation |
| "Dissolves in under 3 seconds" | ⚠️ Needs testing | Performance claim — needs lab validation |

### 3. Labelling Requirements (UK Food Supplements Regulations 2003)

Required on all packaging:
- [ ] Name of the food supplement
- [ ] Names and amounts of nutrients
- [ ] Recommended daily portion
- [ ] Warning not to exceed stated dose
- [ ] Statement that supplements should not replace a varied diet
- [ ] "Keep out of reach of children"
- [ ] Batch number and best before date
- [ ] Manufacturer name and address
- [ ] Net quantity
- [ ] Storage conditions
